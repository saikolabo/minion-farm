Is your mic picking up your PC fan, passing traffic outside, or every click of your keyboard? Poor audio quality can completely ruin an otherwise solid stream or recording. The good news: artificial intelligence now makes it possible to **remove background noise in real time**, no recording studio or expensive gear required. Here are the best free tools to get clean audio, whether you're a gamer, streamer, or content creator.

---

## Why AI Is a Game-Changer for Noise Removal

For a long time, filtering background noise meant either splurging on a high-end microphone or wrestling with expensive, complex plugins. AI changed everything by learning to **distinguish human speech from unwanted noise** with remarkable accuracy.

Unlike traditional filters that bluntly cut certain frequencies — often at the cost of making your voice sound unnatural — AI-based solutions analyze the audio signal in real time and isolate exactly what matters: your voice. The result sounds far more natural, even in noisy environments. And some of these solutions are completely free or offer very generous freemium plans.

---

## Krisp: The Go-To Tool That Works with Any Microphone

[Krisp](https://krisp.ai/) is arguably the most popular noise-removal tool among streamers and remote workers alike. The concept is straightforward: it installs as a **virtual audio device** on Windows or Mac. Once set up in Discord, OBS, Teams, or any other app, it filters background noise on the fly.

**What the free plan includes:**
- 60 minutes of noise suppression per week
- Filtering of both incoming AND outgoing noise
- Compatible with any microphone, even basic headsets

That's plenty to try out the technology or handle short sessions. For an active streamer, the weekly limit can get restrictive quickly — but it's a great fit for casual creators or anyone who wants to evaluate the tool before committing to a subscription.

**Key strength:** Krisp works on any hardware. You don't need a powerful GPU or a high-end PC to run it.

---

## NVIDIA Broadcast: The Ultimate Option for RTX Users

If your PC has an NVIDIA RTX graphics card (2060 or higher), you have access to one of the best noise suppression tools available — and it's **completely free**. [NVIDIA Broadcast](https://www.nvidia.com/broadcast) leverages your GPU's Tensor Cores to filter noise in real time without touching your CPU resources.

**Features included at no cost:**
- Real-time microphone noise suppression
- Noise removal for incoming audio (your teammates' voices, for example)
- Virtual Background and Eye Contact (a bonus for webcam streamers)

The filtering quality is genuinely outstanding. In head-to-head tests, NVIDIA Broadcast wipes out fan noise, mechanical keyboards, and even background conversations with impressive precision.

**Main limitation:** Only available to NVIDIA RTX GPU owners. If you're running AMD or Intel graphics, this solution simply isn't an option.

---

## Cleanvoice AI: The Best Pick for Podcasters and Post-Production

[Cleanvoice AI](https://cleanvoice.ai/) takes a different approach: instead of working in real time, it processes your **audio files after recording**. This makes it the perfect tool for podcasters, YouTubers, or anyone who edits their content before publishing.

Beyond removing background noise, Cleanvoice stands out for its ability to:
- Automatically cut filler words like *uh*, *um*, and other verbal tics
- Clean up lip smacks and mouth sounds
- Detect and trim overly long silences

The free plan includes **30 minutes of audio processing** to test the service — enough to run one or two short episodes through it and judge the results for yourself.

**When should you choose Cleanvoice over Krisp or NVIDIA Broadcast?** When final quality matters more than speed. For a live stream or a call, real-time processing is essential. But for a YouTube video or podcast episode, Cleanvoice's post-processing can genuinely elevate your audio.

## How to Pick the Right Tool for Your Needs

Here's a quick breakdown to help you decide:

| Situation | Recommended Solution |
|---|---|
| Live streaming or gaming | Krisp (any PC) or NVIDIA Broadcast (RTX) |
| PC without an NVIDIA GPU | Krisp |
| Podcast / YouTube | Cleanvoice AI |
| Strictly zero budget | NVIDIA Broadcast (if RTX) or Krisp (free quota) |
| Maximum post-production quality | Cleanvoice AI |

**A few practical tips to get the most out of any of these tools:**
- Even with AI, position your mic 4–6 inches from your mouth for the best signal-to-noise ratio
- Avoid pointing your microphone directly toward your PC tower
- In OBS, add noise suppression as an additional filter alongside Krisp for an extra layer of protection
